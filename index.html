Hello!

I am an Assistant Professor of Cybersecurity and Computer Science at Quinnipiac University's School of Computing and Engineering. I hold a Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering from the University of North Texas and bring a rich background in both academia and industry. Prior to my tenure at Quinnipiac University, I served as an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at Fitchburg State University.

My research interests encompass a wide spectrum of topics, with a primary focus on wireless communication and networks, cybersecurity, and emerging technologies such as 5G, 6G, and beyond networks. In addition to these areas, I am deeply passionate about the application of Machine Learning (ML) and Artificial Intelligence (AI) techniques in network optimization and security. I specialize in network security, vehicular communication, smart cities, Internet of Things (IoT), sensor networks, and the integration of ML and AI for enhancing the efficiency and security of these networks. Over the years, I have designed, implemented, and optimized numerous algorithms, system designs, and hardware architectures tailored for next-generation networks. My contributions to the field include authoring or co-authoring several peer-reviewed papers.

Beyond my research and academic commitments, I remain an active participant in the academic community. I serve on editorial boards and committees for multiple international scientific journals and conferences, making significant contributions to the advancement of my fields of expertise.
